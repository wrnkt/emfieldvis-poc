Background Information

Leading research on the effects of electromagnetic fields, although not comprehensive, suggests that exposure may lead to negative health outcomes. Research suggests that cell phone EMF in the range of ~ 900MHz may cause significant change in the expression of genes, the functioning of the blood-brain barrier, and the gestation/sex of fetuses (in studies of Syrian hamsters). Other research suggests that human endothelial cells have decreased proliferation rates and survivability even under the influence of low-frequency EMF (1 - 5,000 Hz). Observed physiological effects of EMF exposure on humans include insomnia, headache, depression, fatigue, lowered fertility, poor concentration, affected memory, dizziness, irritability, anxiety, nausea, and strange sensations in the skin such as burning and tingling.
